Who are we?

We are a veteran-owned threat assessment consulting firm, dedicated to helping individuals, businesses, and organizations assess and mitigate potential threats to their safety and security. Our team consists of two former Marines who bring years of experience in the field of threat assessment and risk management. At our core, we believe that every individual and organization deserves to feel safe and secure in their environment. We offer a variety of services, including threat assessments, security consulting, risk management, and crisis management, to help our clients achieve this goal.

Our approach is based on a deep understanding of the latest trends in security and risk management, as well as the unique needs and challenges of each of our clients. We work closely with our clients to develop customized solutions that meet their specific needs and budget, while also providing ongoing support and guidance to ensure the success of their security programs. Whether you are a small business owner, a large corporation, or an individual concerned about your personal safety, we are here to help. Contact us today to learn more about our services and how we can help you assess and manage your security risks.

What we do

Threat Assessment

A comprehensive evaluation of potential risks and threats to an individual or organization's safety and security. This may include assessing threats from individuals, groups, or external events, and developing strategies to mitigate these risks.

Security Consulting

service involves a review of an individual or organization's existing security measures and protocols, identifying vulnerabilities, and recommending solutions to improve overall security.

Risk Management

Identifying potential risks and hazards that an individual or organization may face and developing strategies to mitigate or avoid those risks. This may include developing emergency response plans, training staff on risk management, and implementing proactive security measures.

Crisis Management

Developing and implementing protocols and procedures to manage crises, such as natural disasters, cyber-attacks, or acts of violence. This includes developing communication plans, identifying key personnel, and training staff on crisis response protocols.
